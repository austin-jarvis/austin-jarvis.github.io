Near the construction site of the new Energy Engineering Building at the heart of University of Texas in Austinâ€™s campus, there is an intersection of two major campus axes. The north-south path bisects the many buildings that compose the engineering department, and terminates in a pedestrian bridge entering the second story of the new building. Beneath this bridge a footpath carries students uphill from the Arts & Music departments on the east side of campus to the Sciences on the west. At this convergence of paths two objects intervene to stitch the sectional differences together.

These two diaphanous volumes mediate the section of the bridge, extending both above and below the concrete slab. Each box is composed entirely of glass and appears to float within the concrete slab. When standing in the right place, a viewer will see through a hole in the bridge, but elsewhere a periscopic reflection of the enveloping glass will overlay up, down, above, below, sky, and ground.
