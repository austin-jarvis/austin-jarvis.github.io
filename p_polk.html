Arts Management 2016-2018 Houston, TX Client: Polk Street Studios

The Mission Revival Style is a form of architecture unique to the American West. More than three hundred years after the first Spanish missions, buildings like the Rufus Cage school boast design elements still relevant to contemporary architecture. The design in- tention of this project seeks to go beyond the requirements of the City’s Historic Preserva- tion Manual for restoring an historic landmark and to extrapolate the lessons of the Mission Revival Style throughout the design of the site.

The roots of the style were conceived in the early settlements of the Spanish mission- aries exploring California in the 18th century. These settlers devised a form of building that was reminiscent of the Spanish architecture they had left behind but newly adapt- ed to their western environment, rudimentary materials, and simple construction tech- niques. The Rufus Cage school’s original brickwork, structural arches, smoothed stucco, timber roof, and high ceilings all tell a story of the building materials and construction techniques available to the Spanish missionaries of the 1700’s. This is not a poor match for the simple cinder block and steel-truss construction of the warehouses adjacent to the Rufus Cage school. Each building reflects a different era of simplicity and practicality in architecture.

Our vision for the Rufus Cage project is to create a layered site that accommodates diverse experiences. In its final form, the various buildings on the site will work in tandem to create a rich learning experience for all-comers. The warehouses on the Northern edge of the site serves as a focused workspace for students and teachers. The historic school house is the most visible element and will serve as the primary public contact point. The ground floor of the landmark building will act as a public gallery with classrooms on the second floor. The cafeteria building behind the school will be preserved and converted into an auditorium for lectures and presentations. The primary new element will be a courtyard that ties these three buildings together.
