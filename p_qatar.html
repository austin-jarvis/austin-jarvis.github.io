Education City Visitors Centre 2014 Doha, Qatar NADAAA

The typical university's visitor center is a straightforward program. It is typically a place of departure for walking tours, and its primary physical medium is a pamphlet or map. A larger institution of learning, however, requires something different. The Qatar Foundationâ€™s Education City in Doha operates on a scale apart from the usual Western university. It is an amalgam of learning institutions, containing branches of thirteen different colleges and universities including Georgetown, A&M, and Cornell to name a few - a campus of campuses. A visitor here requires more than a pamphlet.

The ambition of this place necessitates something closer in scale and magnitude to a museum. Flexible exhibition is one of the key features. A rotunda that serves simultaneously as an introspective exhibition hall and a panoramic viewing platform mixes media - blurring the line between the exhibition and its subject. The floating circular showcase is positioned to give equal attention to the archeological excavation on the site below and the menagerie of new architectural works beyond. The timeline of past and present is materially manifest. Outside a field of paths carry the visitor through earthen structures, under hanging gardens, and to academic halls beyond.
