Seaholm Transformer Site 2015 Austin, TX NADAAA

Sarah Oppenheimer’s work challenges how we interact with and perceive the buildings we occupy. We are used to a certain amount of agency - opening doors, rearranging furniture, drawing the curtains - and in an institution like a museum we are relegated to a mere observer. A number of recent pieces deemed “switches” have sought to expand the occupant’s agency while drawing attention to architectural order.
S-334473 is a re-installation of a piece originally designed for Peter Eisenman’s Wexner Center for the Arts. In their original context the pivoting glass boxes negotiated the dual-grid of the famous Eisenman floor plan, but in a new context they play a different role. Mass MoCA, a vast contemporary art museum in a century-old industrial space, is a forest of steel columns enclosed in brick and glass. In the corridor where the installation is housed, views of the rolling Berkshire mountains dominate the experience. As the museum visitor activates each of these three-ton glass volumes - rotating them with a touch - the transform from a vertical column-like position to a horizontal landscape orientation where they are oriented to reflect the scenic landscape outside.
