Kaihui Exchange 2018 Changsha, Hunan Province, China WW Architecture

The intended use of the Kaihui Exchange was an evasive subject at the time of commission. The client is a private developer commissioning a building that would serve public interests that were, as yet, unknown. The initial prompt was to make a building that could be a resource for the natural, cultural, and business interests of Kaihui, a town of just 19,500 in rural agrarian China. The result is a building that acts as a frame for a collage of programs - museum, office, restaurant, venue, visitor center in one form.

The approach in the face of this programmatic ambiguity was to morph a modernistic trope (the open floor plan) with a variety of spatial interventions (terraces and courtyards) producing a box that toed the line of inside and outside. Finishing materials and curtains are used as the primary determinant of space, and opaque walls are inserted only where absolutely necessary. The resulting architecture seeks not to predetermine its use, but instead caters to ambiguity. A collection of rooms, each of varying sizes and proportions, tentatively enclosed.

Personal involvement: designer on DD phase, interiors package, custom furniture design, & press materials.
