Seaholm Transformer Site 2015 Austin, TX NADAAA

The Olympics is an urban cannibal. It devours cities, utilizing nooks, crannies, plazas, and squares to create a live-in spectacle. As Paris bids for the 2024 Olympics it takes on the same curatorial challenge as every previous Olympic host. What parts of the City of Lights will be put on display? What parts will remain untouched? How will history inform these decisions?

This project proposes that the 2024 Olympics expand upon the most recognizable event space in Paris - the Eiffel Tower - by converting it into the Olympic Village.

The Eiffel Tower possesses two distinct formal legibilities: the external and the internal. Its external identity is defined by the Tower’s silhouette. The tapering, sleek profile is the symbol of Paris and one of the most recognizable icons in the world. Conversely, the internal identity of the tower is a gaping volume of negative space. When standing beneath the tower at its center and looking up the structure opens up and its scale becomes insurmountable. A colossal void is discovered at the center of Paris and the gestalt of the exterior dissolves as the constituent structural members dominate our experience. To effectively exploit this dual-identity that makes the Eiffel Tower such a profound design any addition must preserve the Tower’s silhouette and accentuate the interior void.

Once the boundary of intervention has been defined, the next stage of analysis looks to the structural module of the box truss. This project fills the outer perimeter of the Tower, conforming to existing connection points and bending to the convention of Eiffel’s irregular taper. A new face of horizontal striations fill out the Tower’s edge. These additions - functionally habitable bridges - exist as parallel pairs, which alternate from level to level, creating a rhythmic reading of the Tower: solid, void, solid, void. Housing for the Olympic athletes is interspersed with public exhibition modules, roof gardens, and communal amenity modules all connected by an inner ring of catwalks looping around the Tower’s preexisting atrium.

At the base of the Tower a colossal excavation creates a new landscape that brings the bank of the Seine beneath Eiffel. Utilizing the river as infrastructure a fleet of boats will transport Olympians from the Tower to the sports venues draped along the Seine throughout greater Paris.
