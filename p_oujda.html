Stade d'Oujda 2016 Oujda, Morocco WW Architecture

A soccer club in an isolated Moroccan city. A stadium in the wild. Two scales overlapping - the camaraderie of sport in the vastness of a desert landscape. The contrast between these two scales is not resolved, no, it is left exposed so that each may enhance the other. For the stadium and desert to intermingle a horizontal slot is opened in the middle of the stands, creating an upper and lower bowl. This void takes on various identities: a portal; a window; a meeting place; a viewing platform; a promenade; a landform.

The network of infrastructure around the stadium is composed of several micro-landscapes. The act of approach is punctuated with many instances of arrival. Parking, streets, gardens, pavilions, fields, and theaters nest into one another. The greater site provides all scales of event space so that the grounds will be equally animated between matches as when the stadium is filled. The base of the stadium is sliced by wide entry points to allow views and activity to bleed between the event and its context.

This room for 45,000 people is in constant flux: a floating object from afar; a room with a view from within. A gathering space in the desert. A line on the horizon.
